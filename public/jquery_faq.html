10 FAQ's About National Parks
  1. When is the park open?
    2. The park is open year-round although many facilities (including visitor centers and ranger stations), concessioners and roads close for the winter season.
  2. How much does it cost to enter the park?
    3. The entrance fees are $25 for a private, noncommercial vehicle; $20 for a motorcycle; or $12 for each visitor 16 years and older entering by foot, bicycle, ski, etc.
  3. Can I bring my pet into the park?
    4. Pets are allowed inside the park, but they must be restrained at all times and are not permitted on hiking trails, inside visitor centers or other facilities.
  4. Where can I get information after I have arrived in the park?
    5. The several visitor centers and one ranger station inside the park are the best source for information about the park.
  5. What do I do if I have an emergency in the park?
    6. Call 911!
  6. What wildlife can I see in the park?
    7. Moose, black and grizzly bears, elk, gray wolves, coyotes and bison.
  7. How long does it take to visit the whole park?
    8. A whole lifetime. You should probably just explore different parts on different visits.
  8. What lodging and campgrounds are availabe in the park?
    9. There are dorm-style rooms and cabins to full-service resort hotels.
  9. What kinds of activities are available in the park?
    10. Fishing, boating, hiking, and climbing at the visitors centers.
  10. When does the park close?
    11. The park entrance gates do not close, but hours vary for park facilities, concessioners and visitor centers.
